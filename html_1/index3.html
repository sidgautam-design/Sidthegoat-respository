WHAT IS PHISHING?
THE ATTACK

A phishing attack is a fake email (or text, call, or website) that pretends to be a trusted company like Amazon, your bank, or Apple — to trick you into handing over your password.

No technical hacking needed. Just a convincing lie. It's the #1 way accounts get broken into.

WHY IT WORKS

Every phishing email uses three psychological tricks:

URGENCY → "Act in 24 hours or lose access!"
FEAR → "Unauthorized $239 charge detected"
AUTHORITY → "Your account team at Amazon"

These combine to make you click before you think.

🚩 RED FLAGS TO SPOT
  • The sender email is slightly wrong — "amaz0n" has a zero
  • Subject is urgent or scary — "Act now!" or "Account suspended"
  • Greets you as "Dear Customer", not your real name
  • The link goes to a strange URL — hover it before clicking
  • Asks for your password — real companies never do this by email
🛡 HOW TO STAY SAFE
  • Never click email links. Go directly to the website yourself.
  • Check every character of the sender's email address.
  • Use a password manager — it won't fill in on a fake site.
  • Turn on 2-factor login — stolen passwords still can't get in.
  • When unsure, call the company on their real number.
